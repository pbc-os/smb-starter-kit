# Creative Multiplication Framework — Full Reference

> **Created by [Curtis Howland](https://www.linkedin.com/in/curtishowland/)** — based on $30M+ in Meta ad spend post-Andromeda. Original framework published on LinkedIn.

## The 3x3x3 Matrix

Three dimensions, 27 unique creative concepts. Each cell in the matrix produces a distinct ad that avoids auction overlap in Meta's algorithm.

---

## Dimension 1: Messaging Angles

### Pain Point

Lead with the problem the audience already feels.

| Sub-Angle | Example Hook | Psychological Triggers |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| Frustration | "Still dealing with X after trying everything?" | Embarrassment, loss aversion |
| Fear | "Your competitors figured this out 6 months ago" | Status anxiety, urgency |
| Regret | "How much did that last agency cost you?" | Loss aversion, sunk cost |

### Desire

Lead with the outcome the audience wants.

| Sub-Angle | Example Hook | Psychological Triggers |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| Aspiration | "Imagine checking your dashboard and seeing this" | Ambition, possibility |
| Freedom | "What would you do with an extra $40K/month?" | Independence, relief |
| Status | "This is what 3x ROAS looks like at scale" | Envy, social comparison |

### Social Proof

Lead with evidence that others have succeeded.

| Sub-Angle | Example Hook | Psychological Triggers |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| Testimony | "We switched from X and cut CPA by 34% in 2 weeks" | Trust, credibility |
| Authority | "10,000 DTC brands use this. Here's why" | FOMO, bandwagon effect |
| Relatability | "I was skeptical too. Then I saw our Q4 numbers" | Validation, identification |

## Dimension 2: Creative Formats

### Static Images

- Drive 60-70% of conversions for most brands
- Smartphone-quality beats studio 84% of the time in Stories
- Fastest to produce, easiest to iterate — test 10 statics in the time it takes to shoot 1 video
- Best when the value is immediately visible (fashion, jewelry, food)
- Decision rule: if the customer needs to SEE IT, run statics

### Video

- First 3 seconds decide everything — track hook rate (3-sec views / impressions)
- Hook rate benchmarks: below 25% kill it, 30-40% good, above 40% scale it
- Partnership Ads (running from creator's handle) = 53% lower CAC
- Best when the customer needs to believe it before they buy (skincare, supplements, transformations)
- Decision rule: if the customer needs to BELIEVE IT, run video

### Carousel

- Best for education-heavy products and comparison selling
- Card 1 hooks with a problem or question, NOT the product
- Average swipe-through on a good carousel = 1.4x higher engagement than single image
- Best when the buyer needs to understand WHY before they buy
- If carousels don't apply, substitute animated statics, GIFs, or another format

## Dimension 3: Funnel Stages

### TOF — Top of Funnel (Awareness)

- Audience: they don't know you
- Strategy: lead with a problem, bold claim, or pattern interrupt
- Volume: 80% of creative volume should live here
- Goal: stop the scroll and create awareness

### MOF — Mid Funnel (Consideration)

- Audience: they've seen you, they're comparing options
- Strategy: education, competitor comparisons, "why us" content
- Best formats: carousels and founder stories work hardest here
- Goal: build preference and consideration

### BOF — Bottom of Funnel (Conversion)

- Audience: they're warm, they need a push
- Strategy: offers, urgency, risk removal, testimonials with CTA overlays
- Tactics: bundles + free gifts work really well here
- Goal: convert intent into purchase

## Why This Works

Meta's algorithm (Andromeda) penalizes redundancy. Similar ads compete against each other in the auction. By systematically varying angle, format, and funnel stage, each of the 27 creatives occupies its own lane — maximizing delivery efficiency and reducing creative fatigue.
